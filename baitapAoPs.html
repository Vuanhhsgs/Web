Problem 1
Extremely Hard
(Teacher Tran Quang Hung) Given a triangle \(ABC\) inscribed in a circle \(\omega\). Choose a point \(P\) on \(\omega\) such that \(AP\) is parallel to \(BC\). The \(A\)-mixtilinear incircle of \(ABC\) is \(\Omega\), which is tangent to \(\omega\) at \(D\). The two tangents from \(P\) to \(\Omega\) intersect \(AD\) at \(M\) and \(N\), with \(M\) lying between \(A\) and \(D\). Prove that two lines \(NB\) and \(CM\) intersect on \(\omega\).

Problem 3
Geometric Number Theory
(Teacher Tran Quang Hung, problem for the IMO Vietnam 2025 Team) Let \(ABCD\) be a square with point \(P\) lying on side \(AB\). Let \(K\) and \(L\) be the centers of the Euler circles of triangles \(PAC\) and \(PBD\), respectively. Suppose that \(PA\) and \(PB\) are positive integers. Prove that \[ \frac{32}{3}\left(AL^4 + BK^4 + KL^4\right) \] is a perfect square.
