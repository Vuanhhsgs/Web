Problems from Vietnamese Euclidean Geometry Forum that I solved

Problem 1
Hard
(Nguyen Hoang Nam) Given \( \triangle ABC \) with circumcenter \( O \). A line through \( O \) meets \( AB \) and \( AC \) at \( E \) and \( F \), respectively. Let \( H \) and \( K \) be the orthocenters of \( \triangle ABC \) and \( \triangle AEF \), respectively. The line \( KH \) meets \( EF \) at \( P \). Let \( Q \) be the isogonal conjugate of \( P \) with respect to \( \triangle ABC \). Prove that \( HQ \parallel OP. \)

Define \(Y\) and \(Z\) similarly. a) Prove that \(MX, NY, PZ\) are concurrent at \(K\). b) Prove that \(OK\) passes through the Lemoine point of \(\triangle ABC\).

Problem 3
Hard
(Teacher Nguyen Van Linh) Let \(\triangle ABC\) be inscribed in \((O)\) with orthocenter \(H\). Point \(X\) lies on segment \(HO\) such that \(HX = \tfrac{1}{2}OX\). Let \(L\) be the Lemoine point of \(\triangle ABC\). The line through \(H\) parallel to the bisector of \(\angle XLO\) meets the line through \(B\) parallel to \(AC\) and the line through \(C\) parallel to \(AB\) at points \(B_1\) and \(C_1\), respectively. Points \(B_2\) and \(C_2\) are the reflections of \(B_1\) over \(B\) and \(C_1\) over \(C\), respectively. Prove that line \(B_2C_2\) is tangent to the circumcircle \((O)\).
